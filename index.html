Band inversion and gap closing

Band inversion alone does not imply a linear crossing between the valence and conduction bands. A linear gap closing requires additional assumptions, such as a single-mass Dirac description and the absence of symmetry-allowed independent mass terms.

For example, consider a Dirac-type Hamiltonian

\[ H(k)=v k\Gamma_1+m_1\Gamma_2+m_2\Gamma_3, \qquad \{\Gamma_i,\Gamma_j\}=2\delta_{ij}. \]

Its spectrum is

\[ E_\pm(k)=\pm\sqrt{v^2k^2+m_1^2+m_2^2}. \]

Therefore, changing the sign of $m_1$ does not close the gap as long as $m_2\neq0$. A gap closing occurs only when the mass vector passes through the origin,

\[ (m_1,m_2)=(0,0). \]

Thus, the implication “band inversion $\Rightarrow$ linear gap closing” is not valid as a general statement. It is valid only under additional restrictions that reduce the relevant mass space to a single direction.
